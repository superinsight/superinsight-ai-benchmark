**SECTION F - RESIDUAL FUNCTIONAL CAPACITY (RFC) ASSESSMENT**

ASSESSMENT DATE: November 5, 2023
ADJUDICATOR: K. Williams, Disability Analyst
MEDICAL CONSULTANT: R. Desai, MD

The claimant has the residual functional capacity to perform sedentary work. He can lift and/or carry 10 pounds frequently and 20 pounds occasionally. He can stand and/or walk (with normal breaks) for a total of about 4 hours in an 8-hour workday. He can sit (with normal breaks) for a total of about 6 hours in an 8-hour workday. He can never climb ladders, ropes, or scaffolds. He can occasionally climb ramps and stairs, balance, stoop, kneel, crouch, and crawl. He must avoid concentrated exposure to hazards such as machinery and unprotected heights.

RATIONALE: The medical evidence supports the conclusion that the claimant's impairments are severe but do not meet or equal a listing. The claimant has a history of significant musculoskeletal issues. The imaging from 2013-03-15 (Exhibit 1F) showing a complex rotator cuff tear and cervical/lumbar disc herniations establishes long-standing degenerative changes that would support limitations in overhead reaching and lifting.

The primary impairment is severe osteoarthritis of the right hip. The evidence from 2022-09-15 (Exhibit 2F) documents significant pre-operative findings, including a diagnosis of avascular necrosis and radiographic evidence of arthritis. The claimant subsequently underwent a right total hip arthroplasty on 2023-02-08 (Exhibit 3F), which is a significant surgical procedure. The post-operative course appears favorable, as noted in the follow-up visit on 2023-04-13 (Exhibit 2F), where the claimant reported being happy with his progress and was no longer using pain medication. However, he was still using a cane at that time, and the consultative examination in October 2023 still shows some residual weakness and ROM deficits in the operative hip. The post-op imaging (Exhibit 5F) confirms good placement of the prosthesis. The pre-operative cardiac evaluation on January 20, 2023 (Exhibit 4F) showed normal heart function with an LVEF of 55%, indicating no cardiac limitations.

The claimant's other documented conditions, including hypertension and the breast lump/cervicalgia noted on 2023-07-06 (Exhibit 6F), are considered non-severe at this time as they do not appear to impose any significant functional limitations beyond those already assessed. The RFC is consistent with the opinion of the consultative examiner (Exhibit 7F) and is supported by the totality of the evidence. The claimant is limited to sedentary work.
