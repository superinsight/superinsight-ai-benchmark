This form is to confirm your understanding of the risks associated with clozapine and the requirements of the Clozapine Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS) Program.

**Key Risks of Clozapine:**
-   **Severe Neutropenia:** A serious blood condition where you have a low number of neutrophils, a type of white blood cell that fights infection. This can be life-threatening.
-   **Orthostatic Hypotension, Bradycardia, Syncope:** Dizziness, low blood pressure, slow heart rate, and fainting, especially when starting the medication or increasing the dose.
-   **Seizures**
-   **Myocarditis and Cardiomyopathy:** Inflammation or disease of the heart muscle.

**REMS Program Requirements:**
-   You must be enrolled in the Clozapine REMS Program to receive this medication.
-   You must have your Absolute Neutrophil Count (ANC) checked regularly through a blood test.
-   Your ANC must be checked *before* you can receive your next supply of clozapine.
-   Your pharmacy cannot dispense clozapine unless your ANC is within the acceptable range and has been reported to the REMS program.
