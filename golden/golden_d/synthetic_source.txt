**SECTION F: ASSESSMENT OF RESIDUAL FUNCTIONAL CAPACITY (RFC)**

**PHYSICAL RFC ASSESSMENT:**
The claimant is found to have the residual functional capacity to perform light work. He can lift and/or carry 20 pounds occasionally and 10 pounds frequently. He can stand and/or walk for a total of about 4 hours in an 8-hour workday. He can sit for about 6 hours in an 8-hour workday, however, he requires a sit/stand option allowing for a change of position at least every 30 minutes to alleviate discomfort from his lumbar condition. The claimant should never climb ladders, ropes, or scaffolds. He can occasionally climb ramps and stairs, balance, stoop, kneel, crouch, and crawl. He must avoid concentrated exposure to hazards such as unprotected heights and dangerous machinery due to potential distraction from pain and medication side effects.

This assessment is supported by the overall medical record. While the claimant's allegations of total disability are noted, the objective evidence does not fully support such severe limitations. For example, the orthopedic evaluation on 2021-03-22 (Exhibit 2F) showed 5/5 motor strength and improved range of motion after injections. The consultative examination on 2024-01-10 (Exhibit 10F) also documented 5/5 motor strength, negative straight leg raising, and the ability to ambulate without an assistive device. The need for a sit/stand option is consistent with documentation from Lakewood Family Medicine on Nov 10, 2020, restricting prolonged sitting. The lifting restriction of 15-20 pounds is consistent with recommendations from Dr. Greene on 22-Mar-21 and the light exertional level assessed by Dr. Hargrove.

**MENTAL RFC ASSESSMENT:**
The claimant is capable of understanding, remembering, and carrying out simple, routine tasks. He can maintain attention and concentration for two-hour periods. He can interact appropriately with supervisors and coworkers on an occasional basis. He is able to adapt to routine workplace changes. He should avoid work involving frequent interaction with the general public.

This assessment is supported by the mental health records. The evaluation of November 2nd, 2021 (Exhibit 4F) showed a constricted affect and worried mood, but also a linear and goal-directed thought process with intact cognition. Follow-up notes from his PCP (Exhibits 1F) indicate his mood improved with medication adjustments, and the consultative examination (Exhibit 10F) yielded PHQ-9 and GAD-7 scores in the mild range, suggesting his conditions are reasonably well-managed. The limitation on public interaction is a reasonable accommodation for his documented anxiety. He is not precluded from all work activity on a mental basis.

**VOCATIONAL ANALYSIS:**
Considering the claimant's age, education, work experience, and residual functional capacity, he is unable to perform his past relevant work as a warehouse laborer, which is classified as heavy, semi-skilled work. However, using the RFC as a basis, there are other jobs that exist in significant numbers in the national economy that the claimant can perform, such as assembler (light), mail clerk (light), or ticket taker (sedentary). Therefore, a finding of "not disabled" is warranted.
